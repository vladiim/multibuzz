# Citation Sources for mbuzz Articles

All statistics in mbuzz Academy articles should be properly cited. This document contains verified sources for common claims.

---

## Core Attribution Statistics

### 1. Dark Funnel / Untrackable Influences (20-50%)

**Primary Source: McKinsey & Company**
> "Word of mouth is the primary factor behind 20 to 50 percent of all purchasing decisions."

- **Source:** McKinsey - A new way to measure word-of-mouth marketing
- **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/a-new-way-to-measure-word-of-mouth-marketing
- **Year:** 2010
- **Use in articles:** Dark funnel, word-of-mouth attribution

---

### 2. iOS ATT Opt-In Rates

**Primary Source: Adjust (Q2 2025)**
> Industry-wide average opt-in rate is 35% for users shown the prompt. Immediate prompt opt-in rates are only 13-14%.

- **Source:** Adjust - ATT opt-in rates 2025
- **URL:** https://www.adjust.com/blog/att-opt-in-rates-2025/
- **Year:** 2025

**Supporting Source: Singular (2024)**
> "ATT opt-in rates declined to just 13.85% globally for immediate prompts"

- **URL:** https://www.singular.net/blog/att-opt-in-rates-2024/

**Note:** Original "20-25%" claim is outdated. Update articles to use 13-35% range depending on prompt strategy.

### 6. Multi-Channel Customer Journeys (73%)

- **Context:** Survey of 43,000 shoppers
- **URL:** https://gitnux.org/multi-channel-marketing-statistics/

### 7. Platform Over-Attribution (1.5x-3x)

**Primary Source: Haus (Meta Study)**
> Platform reports 1.5x ROAS, but true incremental ROAS is only 0.9x (meaning 40%+ of attributed revenue would have happened anyway)

- **URL:** https://www.haus.io/article/meta-incrementality-testing
- **Year:** 2024

**Primary Source: Haus (Google Ads Study)**
> "Turning off a campaign would only decrease total sales by 30% of what Google attributes to it. The remaining 70% represents sales that would have happened anyway."

- **URL:** https://www.haus.io/article/google-ads-incrementality-testing

**Supporting Source: Measured**
> "Google's attribution is inherently biased toward Google's own products."

- **URL:** https://www.measured.com/faq/google-platform-attribution-pros-cons-and-why-you-need-more/

### 8. GA4 Data-Driven Attribution Requirements

**Primary Source: Google Analytics Help**
> "The current threshold is: 400 conversions per conversion type with a path length of 2+ interactions"

- **URL:** https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/3070276?hl=en
- **Note:** This was Universal Analytics requirement. GA4 removed hard minimum but recommends 400 for optimal DDA performance.

**Supporting Source: Cardinal Path**
> "GA4 recommends at least 400 conversions within 28 days for DDA to be effective."

- **URL:** https://www.cardinalpath.com/blog/an-overview-of-data-driven-attribution-in-ga4

**Supporting Source: Napkyn**
- **URL:** https://www.napkyn.com/blog/15-common-ga4-attribution-challenges-and-how-to-solve-them

**Primary Source: Databox**

- **URL:** https://databox.com/b2b-sales-cycle-length
- **Year:** 2024

**Supporting Source: Gartner**
> "Enterprise deals are typically executed over longer sales cycles (six months or more), for larger deal sizes (greater than $125,000)"

- **URL:** https://www.gartner.com/en/sales/insights/b2b-buying-journey

- > $100,000 ACV: 3-9 months

- **URL:** https://blog.hellostepchange.com/blog/the-industry-average-b2b-sales-cycle-length

## Academic Citations

### Shapley Value Attribution

**Original Paper:**
> Shapley, L. S. (1953). "A Value for n-Person Games." In Kuhn, H. and Tucker, A., Eds., *Contributions to the Theory of Games II*, Princeton University Press, Princeton, pp. 307-317.

- **PDF:** https://www.rand.org/content/dam/rand/pubs/papers/2021/P295.pdf
- **DOI:** https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/9781400881970-018/html
- **Nobel Prize:** Lloyd Shapley won the 2012 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences for this work.

**Google's Application:**
- **URL:** https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/3191594

---

### Markov Chain Attribution

**Foundational Paper:**
> Anderl, E., Becker, I., von Wangenheim, F., & Schumann, J. H. (2016). "Mapping the customer journey: Lessons learned from graph-based online attribution modeling." *International Journal of Research in Marketing*, 33(3), 457-474.

- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijresmar.2016.03.001
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167811616300349
- **ResearchGate:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/299131474_Mapping_the_customer_journey_Lessons_learned_from_graph-based_online_attribution_modeling
